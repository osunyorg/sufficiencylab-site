Conference 5:30-7pm at Académie du Climat, Paris  

body: >-


What if sufficiency shaped the policies of Paris?  How would the city look, function, and evolve? Join us for a bold and inspiring event where  CARE student teams  unveil  innovative,  research-driven proposals  for transforming urban life-rethinking digitalization, food systems, trade, and urban living through the principle of  doing better with less . As climate and social pressures escalate, sufficiency is gaining momentum, challenging outdated assumptions about growth and consumption. This event showcases how Paris can lead the way in redefining resource use-ensuring sustainability, equity, and resilience for all. After  five months of collaborative research , international student teams will present their visionary strategies for embedding sufficiency into Parisian policymaking. Grounded in  global case studies and field visits , their proposals tackle real-world solutions in key policy areas where rethinking resource dependency is both. The event is co-organized the  World Sufficiency Lab  and the  CARE Programme   (Climate Adaptation, Resilience and Empowerment)  as part of the first-ever Sufficiency Summer School, and hosted at  Académie du Climat .  For more information about activities of  Académie du Climat , sign up for their newsletter  HERE  .   To register, click  HERE  Event Program: 5:30 PM - 5:40 PM - Opening Remarks ,  Dr. Yamina Saheb   5:40 PM - 6:40 PM -  Students' Presentations 

Digitalisation  research team including  Bahaar Abbas ,  Jan Cipar ,  Kamshajini Raveenthiran .

Nutrition  research team including  Nishi Kant Dixit ,  Diana Mear , Anna Russo Spena and Lovelyn Umeh  

Trade research team including Sam Crosby,  Chang Du ,  Lukas Kortendieck  and  Josie O'Brien


Urban planning research team including  Elyne Fabing ,  Thomas Hegarty ,  Elisabeth Hirtz ,  Roméo Petric ,  Naifah Putri ,  Tasha Romeyn ,  Ailie Ross-Oliver ,  Sophie Seiser ,  Naomi Trick  

6:40 PM - 7:00 PM -  Q&A Session
