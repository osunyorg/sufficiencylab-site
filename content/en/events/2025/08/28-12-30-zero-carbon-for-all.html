A Webinar Launch: August 28, 2025 from 12:30 to 13:30 CET

Zero Carbon for All introduces the first-ever carbon fair-share tool aligned with the responsibility and capability principles established under the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (external link) (UNFCCC) and referenced in the International Court of Justice's July 2025advisory opinion. (external link)While this advisory opinion is not legally binding, it carries significant moral and political weight. It clarifies the obligations of states to act urgently and equitably to limit global warming to 1.5°C.By applying sufficiency as an equity principle to both territorial and consumption-based emissions, the tool provides a timeline for immediate implementation. Thus, offering a practical framework to help states meet the expectations outlined in the ICJ's opinion and deliver on the 1.5°C temperature target.The tool was developed by the World Sufficiency Lab, in collaboration with Data For Good.Agenda of the webinar12:30 - 12:35: Welcome and Opening: YaminaSaheb (external link) (Director, World Sufficiency Lab)12:35 - 12:55: Presentation of the Zero Carbone Tool: (Yamina Saheb (external link), Louis Tronel (external link) and Emma Domingues (external link))12:55 - 13-25: Round Table discussionModerator: Rishika Pardikar (external link), Environment and Climate reporter from IndiaPanelists:Maria Antonia Tigre (external link) (Director, Global Climate Litigation)      Laurence Taubiana (external link) (CEO, European Climate Foundation)Cecilia Knuthia  (external link)(UNFCCC Director)  Wanjira Mathai  (external link)(COP 30: Global Ethical Stocktake)Ines Camilloni (external link) (IPCC Vice-Chair)13:25 - 13:30: Next steps and closing remarks (Yamina Saheb)

A Webinar Launch: August 28, 2025 from 12:30 to 13:30 CET

Zero Carbon for All introduces the first-ever carbon fair-share tool aligned with the responsibility and capability principles established under the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (external link) (UNFCCC) and referenced in the International Court of Justice's July 2025advisory opinion. (external link)While this advisory opinion is not legally binding, it carries significant moral and political weight. It clarifies the obligations of states to act urgently and equitably to limit global warming to 1.5°C.By applying sufficiency as an equity principle to both territorial and consumption-based emissions, the tool provides a timeline for immediate implementation. Thus, offering a practical framework to help states meet the expectations outlined in the ICJ's opinion and deliver on the 1.5°C temperature target.The tool was developed by the World Sufficiency Lab, in collaboration with Data For Good.Agenda of the webinar12:30 - 12:35: Welcome and Opening: YaminaSaheb (external link) (Director, World Sufficiency Lab)12:35 - 12:55: Presentation of the Zero Carbone Tool: (Yamina Saheb (external link), Louis Tronel (external link) and Emma Domingues (external link))12:55 - 13-25: Round Table discussionModerator: Rishika Pardikar (external link), Environment and Climate reporter from IndiaPanelists:Maria Antonia Tigre (external link) (Director, Global Climate Litigation)      Laurence Taubiana (external link) (CEO, European Climate Foundation)Cecilia Knuthia  (external link)(UNFCCC Director)  Wanjira Mathai  (external link)(COP 30: Global Ethical Stocktake)Ines Camilloni (external link) (IPCC Vice-Chair)13:25 - 13:30: Next steps and closing remarks (Yamina Saheb)
