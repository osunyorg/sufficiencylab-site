Zero Carbon for All


Zero Carbon for All  introduces the first-ever carbon fair-share tool aligned with the responsibility and capability principles established under the  United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change  (UNFCCC) and referenced in the  International Court of Justice's July 2025advisory opinion. While the ICJ advisory opinion is not legally binding, it carries significant moral and political weight. It clarifies the obligations of states to act urgently and equitably to limit global warming to 1.5°C. By applying  sufficiency  as an equity principle to both territorial and consumption-based emissions, the tool provides a timeline for immediate implementation. Thus, offering a practical framework to help states meet the expectations outlined in the ICJ's opinion and deliver on the  1.5°C temperature target . The tool was developed by the  World Sufficiency Lab , in collaboration with  Data For Good .
