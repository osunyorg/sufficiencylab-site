About

The Swiss Sufficiency Lab (SSL) is a national hub dedicated to advancing sufficiency as a key pillar of sustainable transformation, rethinking how we live, work, and thrive within planetary boundaries. Hosted by the Urban Energy Systems Laboratory at Empa , and part of the World Sufficiency Lab network, the SSL brings together researchers, policymakers, practitioners, and citizens to make sufficiency a practical and inspiring lever for transforming our societies toward sustainability and well-being. Through research and innovation, cross-sectoral collaboration, and international exchange, the SSL connects people and ideas to co-create sufficiency-based strategies, pilot projects, and real-world solutions. By sharing knowledge, best practices, and stories of change, the SSL aims to unleash our collective imagination, turning sufficiency from a niche concept into a shared pathway toward a thriving, equitable, low-carbon future.

Call to action

A vibrant community of researchers, policymakers, practitioners, and citizens is forming around the Swiss Sufficiency Lab to exchange ideas, share experiences, and co-create pathways toward sustainable, thriving Swiss society. An online space for collaboration and dialogue will launch soon - stay tuned to connect, contribute, and be part of Switzerland’s growing sufficiency movement!
