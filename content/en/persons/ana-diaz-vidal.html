Ana is an environmental policy student currently pursuing a master’s in environmental policy (with concentrations in Methods and Global Economy) at Sciences Po Paris. She has worked for REN21, a renewable energy policy network, focusing on energy research and is currently working at the OECD’s Climate Change Expert Group (CCXG) researching international climate policy. Ana has written a master’s thesis entitled “Summarising is power: The making of the IPCC Summary for Policymakers”, using machine learning to explore the debates at the intersection between science and policy. Ana is interested in post-growth and sufficiency, and in particular in the links between economy and ecology.
