Emile Belembois is PhD candidate at MINES Saint Etienne and the association France Villes et territoires Durable in Paris.He worked for more than a year in the European advocacy department of the négaWatt association, where the 1st EU sufficiency driven scenario, CLEVER, was developed.Emile's research is about the use of the planetary boundaries' framework at the local level in France. He is investigating the systemic vision of environmental and justice issues. This work is part of a regular exchange with researchers at the Stockholm Resilience Centre, where this framework originated.Emile holds a M.Sc. in Civil Engineering and a M.Sc. in Ecological Transition Governance from École Polytechnique and AgroParis.
